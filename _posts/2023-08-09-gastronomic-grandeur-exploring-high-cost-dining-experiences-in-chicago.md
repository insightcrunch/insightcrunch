Chicago's fine dining scene extends well beyond Alinea. A survey of the city's most expensive tables — what you actually get for the price, and an honest accounting of whether the bill is ever justified.

## What makes a meal worth the price

The answer is never just the food. It's the density of attention — the ratio of staff to tables, the number of decisions made on your behalf before you arrive, the architecture of the evening as a designed experience.

## The Chicago argument

Chicago has a stronger claim to fine dining than most American cities care to admit. The city's restaurant culture is serious in a way that's less about prestige and more about craft. The tasting menu scene here rewards curiosity more than it rewards status.

## The honest assessment

Three hours and four hundred dollars for dinner is an experience that most people will have once or twice in their lives if they're lucky. Whether it's worth it depends entirely on what you're paying attention to. Those who arrive distracted get the same food as those who arrive present — and a fundamentally different meal.
