## Overview

The Tata Consultancy Services National Qualifier Test (NQT) is taken by hundreds of thousands of aspiring candidates every year. A successful TCS NQT candidate qualifies for various different profiles in TCS like the TCS Digital, TCS Ninja, and more. The tagging of a candidate to the appropriate profile is done based on the score achieved by the candidate in the TCS NQT exam. Hence, it is important to score good marks in the exam to get selected for the most prestigious profiles. ## TCS NQT Cut Off Marks

The TCS NQT Passing Marks is relative based on how the candidates perform and how complex the questions were in the specific test. The number of vacancies in the organization at that time also determines the TCS NQT cut off mark. With an increase in the number of candidates the cut off score also automatically rises. Thus if one particular set of questions the topper scored 75%, then we can expect the cut off score to be below 75%. Same way if the topper in TCS NQT in one set of questions scored 95%, we can expect the passing marks for TCS NQT to be much higher. In this case, I would also suggest this is not generally the case. Because there might be exception cases where someone might score 95%, but the majority of the candidates might have scored much lower. In such a case, the passing marks for the TCS NQT exam will be much lower. The most important thing in such a scenario is to make sure you try to grab every mark possible in every question from every topic. Plan your preparation to be full proof in every section of the syllabus, and take extensive quality practice tests to evaluate yourself.
