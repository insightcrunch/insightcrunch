## AI for Photo Culling and Selection

### The Culling Problem

For event photographers, wedding photographers, and anyone shooting large volumes - culling is the hidden productivity thief. A wedding photographer shooting 2,000 frames over an eight-hour day might spend 4-6 hours culling before editing begins. AI culling tools have cut this time dramatically.

### AI Culling Tools

**Aftershoot Culling:** Trains on your selection preferences over time, learning which sharpness thresholds, expressions, and compositions you favor. After an initial calibration period, Aftershoot pre-selects keepers from a large shoot with 80-90% accuracy, leaving the photographer to review AI selections rather than scan every frame individually. Integrates directly with Lightroom.

**Smart Photo Widget:** AI analysis that scores photos on technical quality (focus, exposure, blur) and identifies groups of similar shots so you can quickly select the best from each burst. Particularly useful for sports and wildlife photographers shooting rapid sequences.

**Narrative Select:** Designed specifically for wedding photographers, Narrative Select identifies the best from each coverage moment (ceremony, first dance, speeches, etc.) while also using facial recognition to ensure good coverage of key people throughout the event.

**Photo Mechanic AI integration:** Photo Mechanic Plus includes AI-powered rating and culling features that speed up the initial selection phase for news, sports, and event photographers who need rapid turnaround.

### The AI Culling Workflow

**Calibration period:** Most AI culling tools require 50-100 manually selected photos to learn your preferences. This initial investment pays off within the first few uses.

**Review, not select:** The mental model shift - from "look at every photo and select keepers" to "review AI selections and correct errors" - reduces culling time far more than the AI accuracy percentage alone suggests. Even at 85% accuracy, reviewing 300 AI-selected keepers from 2,000 frames is vastly faster than scanning all 2,000.

**Second pass efficiency:** Reserve your own careful judgment for the final 20% cut (from selects to the actual delivery set) rather than spending it on the initial cull.

## AI Noise Reduction and Image Enhancement

### Why AI Noise Reduction Changed Everything

Traditional noise reduction (Lightroom's Luminance slider, Photoshop Camera Raw) required trading noise for detail - reducing grain while blurring the fine texture that makes images look sharp. AI noise reduction models have largely solved this trade-off by learning to distinguish noise from actual image detail.

### The Leading AI Noise Reduction Tools

**Lightroom AI Denoise (Enhance > Denoise):** Adobe's AI denoise processes RAW files using a model trained to separate noise from detail. Results are noticeably better than Lightroom's traditional noise reduction, particularly for high ISO images. The process takes 30-60 seconds per image but the quality difference is significant for ISO 3200+ shots.

**Topaz Photo AI:** Combines noise reduction, sharpening, and upscaling in a single AI-powered workflow. Topaz's DeNoise model is widely considered the best standalone noise reduction available, with particularly strong performance on extreme ISO (6400+) wildlife and astrophotography images. Integrates as a Lightroom plugin or runs standalone.

**DxO PhotoLab with DeepPRIME XD:** DxO's AI noise reduction is particularly strong for specific cameras because DxO has trained models on data from individual camera sensors. DeepPRIME XD produces exceptional results for cameras with known noise characteristics.

**Lightroom Classic vs. Topaz Comparison:**
For most photographers shooting events, weddings, or indoor sports, Lightroom AI Denoise now produces excellent results without additional software. For wildlife photographers, astrophotographers, and photographers regularly pushing to ISO 12800+, Topaz Photo AI's more aggressive models are typically worth the additional cost.

### AI Sharpening and Upscaling

**Topaz Gigapixel AI:** AI-powered upscaling that increases image resolution by 2x, 4x, or 6x while adding realistic detail rather than just interpolating existing pixels. Most useful for: wildlife shots where the subject is small in frame, historical image restoration, print upsizing beyond native resolution.

**Lightroom Enhance (Super Resolution):** Adobe's super resolution doubles linear dimensions (4x the pixel count) using AI modeling. Available within Lightroom's existing workflow without additional software. Particularly useful for landscape images being printed at large scale.

**ON1 Resize AI:** Standalone or plugin for AI-powered enlargement with strong results for fine art and commercial print applications.

## AI Portrait Retouching

### AI Skin Retouching Tools

Manual portrait retouching - frequency separation, dodge and burn, blemish removal across dozens of exposures - has historically been the most time-intensive editing task in portrait and wedding photography. AI has dramatically reduced this time burden.

**Lightroom AI Portrait Masking:** Lightroom's AI automatically identifies and masks skin, face, teeth, eyes, and hair separately in portrait photos. This eliminates the most tedious part of portrait editing: creating precise masks for each person in each image.

**Luminar Neo Portrait Tools:**
- **Skin AI:** Automatically retouches skin texture, smooths blemishes, and adjusts skin tone while preserving natural texture
- **Face AI:** Adjusts facial features (slim face, enhance eyes, adjust smile) non-destructively
- **Portrait Bokeh AI:** Adds or enhances bokeh blur behind portraits in situations where the photographer wanted more separation between subject and background

**Portrait Pro:** Specialized portrait retouching software with AI-powered feature detection and adjustment. Best for commercial portrait work where significant retouching is expected and time savings per image are meaningful.

**Adobe Photoshop Neural Filters - Skin Smoothing:** Available within Photoshop for photographers already in the Adobe ecosystem. The Skin Smoothing filter provides AI-powered retouching that can be adjusted for intensity and blended with the original.

### AI Eye and Detail Enhancement

**Topaz Sharpen AI - Face Detection:** Identifies faces in images and applies more aggressive sharpening to facial features (especially eyes) while applying softer sharpening to backgrounds and less critical areas.

**Lightroom Enhance Eyes:** Within Lightroom's AI portrait masking, the eye sub-mask allows targeted brightening and sharpening of irises, which is one of the most impactful quick improvements for portrait quality.

## AI Sky Replacement and Landscape Tools

### Sky Replacement

Replacing dull or overcast skies with dramatic alternatives has moved from expert compositing work to a one-click operation. Both the tool options and the ethical questions around when to use it deserve attention.

**Luminar Neo AI Sky Replacement:** The most powerful and flexible sky replacement tool. Luminar's Sky AI:
- Identifies the sky area automatically regardless of complex foreground elements (trees, buildings, irregular horizons)
- Applies realistic lighting changes to the foreground to match the replacement sky
- Recolors reflections in water automatically
- Includes a substantial library of replacement skies

**Lightroom Masking (AI Sky Mask):** Lightroom's AI sky selection is excellent for targeted adjustments to the sky without full replacement - enhancing an existing sky's drama through selective exposure, color, and texture adjustments.

**Photoshop Sky Replacement:** Built into Photoshop's Edit menu, Photoshop's sky replacement includes lighting adjustment for the foreground scene.

**The ethics of sky replacement:**
Sky replacement sits at the intersection of technical capability and professional ethics. For commercial work (real estate, travel marketing, lifestyle advertising), sky replacement is widely accepted and often expected. For documentary, photojournalistic, and fine art photography, replacing skies crosses ethical lines that should be considered carefully. Professional associations including the NPPA have clear guidelines against sky replacement in news photography.

### AI Landscape Enhancement Tools

**Luminar Neo Structure AI:** Adds micro-contrast and texture to landscape images in a way that enhances the feeling of depth and detail without the halo artifacts that traditional clarity and texture sliders can create.

**Lightroom AI Landscape Mask:** Automatically identifies and separately masks sky, foliage, water, rock, and other landscape elements for targeted adjustments.

**ON1 Photo RAW AI HDR:** AI-powered HDR processing that produces more natural-looking results than traditional HDR by using the AI model to identify and handle motion (swaying trees, water movement) between exposures.

**Powerline and Object Removal:**
For landscape photographers, the presence of powerlines, tourist crowds, and other unwanted elements in otherwise perfect shots is a persistent frustration. AI object removal has become practical enough to use routinely:

**Lightroom Content-Aware Remove:** Adobe's AI-powered removal tool in Lightroom works on smaller, less complex objects effectively. For powerlines crossing clear sky backgrounds, the results are often excellent.

**Photoshop Generative Fill:** For complex removals (crowds of tourists, large temporary structures, complex backgrounds), Photoshop's Generative Fill uses AI to fill removed areas with contextually appropriate generated content. The results vary by complexity but are often impressive for landscape applications.

## AI for Photoshop

### Photoshop Generative Fill and Expand

Adobe Firefly's integration into Photoshop has added genuinely powerful generative capabilities:

**Generative Fill:** Select any area of an image and generate AI content to fill it. Applications for photographers:
- Extend backgrounds to accommodate different crops or aspect ratios
- Remove unwanted objects and replace with contextually generated background
- Add elements to scenes (additional foliage, different weather elements, styled objects)
- Correct near-misses (extend a frame where a subject was slightly cropped)

**Generative Expand:** Extends the canvas of an image beyond its original borders, generating contextually appropriate content to fill the new areas. Useful for: converting vertical images to horizontal for panoramic displays, adding breathing room to tightly cropped images, creating matching backgrounds for composite work.

**Sky AI and Object Selection:** Photoshop's AI-powered selection tools (Subject, Sky, Object) provide the accurate masking that powers other operations - sky replacement, background removal, targeted adjustments.

**Neural Filters:** The Neural Filters panel includes AI-powered filters for: skin smoothing, colorizing black and white photos, smart portrait (facial feature adjustment), depth blur, and others. Quality varies by filter, with skin smoothing and smart portrait being most practically useful for photographers.

## AI for Wildlife and Nature Photography

### Technical Challenges AI Addresses

Wildlife photography combines the most extreme technical challenges in photography: maximum ISO, maximum shutter speed, maximum telephoto focal lengths, and constantly moving unpredictable subjects:

**In-camera AI subject detection:** Sony, Nikon, and Canon all offer AI-powered animal eye detection in current mirrorless systems. For birds in flight, this has changed what is achievable - the hit rate for sharp bird-in-flight images has increased dramatically for photographers who previously relied entirely on manual tracking skill.

**Post-processing for wildlife:**

**Noise reduction priority:** High ISO is inherent to wildlife photography. Topaz Photo AI is the standard recommendation for wildlife photographers because its noise reduction maintains the fine feather and fur texture that distinguishes excellent wildlife images from technically adequate ones.

**Motion blur recovery:** Topaz Sharpen AI's motion deblur can sometimes recover images with slight motion blur that would otherwise be discarded. Not reliable enough to substitute for proper technique, but valuable for those moments where perfect technique was impossible.

**AI crop and composition:** For wildlife photographers regularly working at maximum focal length, the ability to upscale significantly (Topaz Gigapixel) means a sharp image where the subject is slightly too small can be salvaged through AI upscaling to a usable print size.

**Batch processing for high volumes:** Wildlife photographers on safari or at breeding colonies can shoot thousands of frames per day. Aftershoot and similar culling tools that understand the wildlife context (sharp eyes, good wing position, interesting behavior) handle the initial cut efficiently.

## AI for Street Photography and Documentary Work

### Technical Considerations

Street and documentary photography emphasizes authenticity, which creates specific tensions with AI editing capabilities:

**What AI helps with legitimately:**
Noise reduction for available-light street photography is widely accepted. Subject isolation for compositional clarity can be acceptable. Basic exposure and contrast adjustment is standard.

**What crosses documentary ethics lines:**
Adding or removing people from scenes, sky replacement, generating fictional elements, significant facial retouching that changes expressions - these violate the documentary compact with viewers.

**The practical street photography AI workflow:**
High-ISO noise reduction (essential for available-light work), minimal exposure correction, lens correction, and cropping for composition - these are broadly accepted. Any editing that changes what was actually in the scene crosses a line that most documentary photographers maintain clearly.

## AI for Commercial and Advertising Photography

### Consistency at Scale

Commercial photography often requires consistency across hundreds or thousands of product or model images. AI addresses scale-related consistency challenges:

**Batch skin retouching:** Portrait AI tools (Portrait Pro, Lightroom AI masking + adjustment) can be applied consistently across large shoots with multiple subjects - ensuring uniform treatment across all images in a campaign.

**Background consistency:** Remove.bg or Photoshop's AI subject selection applied to an entire product catalogue ensures consistent background treatment that would be impossible to achieve manually at scale.

**Color grading consistency:** AI-powered Lightroom Sync applies consistent color grading across all images in a series. Combined with AI Auto that standardizes the baseline, large campaigns can be graded consistently without shot-by-shot manual adjustment.

**Creative variation from single shoot:** Photoshop Generative Fill enables creating multiple background variations from a single hero shot - the model in the same pose against different settings for different market regions, seasons, or campaign uses.

### Compositing and Visual Effects

Commercial photography often requires compositing that AI accelerates:

**AI subject extraction for composites:** Clean subject extraction is the foundation of all composite work. Photoshop's AI subject selection and Remove.bg handle 80-90% of extraction needs, with manual cleanup required only for complex hair, transparent materials, and very fine edges.

**Shadow and reflection generation:** Photoshop's AI can generate or extend shadows and reflections that make composites look physically plausible without manual painting.

**Color matching:** AI-powered color matching between composite elements (subject and background shot in different lighting conditions) is more accurate and faster than manual color grading.

## Advanced AI Photo Editing Techniques

### Frequency Separation With AI Assistance

Traditional frequency separation retouching (separating texture and color information for targeted retouching) is time-intensive but produces the most natural-looking skin retouching results. AI tools accelerate the process:

**AI setup:** Create the frequency separation layers automatically using Photoshop actions. Apply Lightroom AI skin masking to identify the retouch area precisely.

**AI texture reference:** Topaz Photo AI's texture preservation in noise reduction can be used to generate a reference of the ideal skin texture that the manual retouching should approach.

**Intelligent healing:** Photoshop's Healing Brush and Clone Stamp now use AI to better match texture when healing skin blemishes, reducing the manual adjustment needed after the initial healing operation.

### Color Grading With AI Starting Points

**AI color grading tools:** Luminar Neo, VSCO, and Lightroom all offer AI-generated starting points for color grades. The Lightroom "Auto" function now generates a starting grade that accounts for image content (landscape vs. portrait) rather than just exposure targets.

**Preset application with AI adjustment:** Smart preset applications that automatically adjust preset strength based on the image's existing characteristics (not applying the same preset strength to both bright and dark images) are becoming standard in professional preset collections.

**Referencing your own style:** Some AI tools now allow training on your own edit history to learn your color preferences and apply them more consistently to new images - a "personal AI preset" based on your actual edits rather than a designed preset.

### AI for Black and White Conversion

**Silver Efex Pro with AI:** The industry standard for black and white conversion, Silver Efex Pro uses AI to make film emulation more accurate and to provide intelligent adaptive processing based on image content.

**Lightroom Black and White AI mixer:** Lightroom's Black and White Mix panel with AI target selection helps photographers adjust which colors become which tones in the conversion.

**Luminosity masking with AI:** Luminosity masks (selections based on tonal values in the image) are powerful for targeted black and white adjustments. AI tools that generate intelligent luminosity masks (Lumenzia, TK Actions) have made this sophisticated technique accessible to photographers without advanced compositing knowledge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the most useful AI tools for photographers?

The highest-impact AI tools depend on your photography specialty. For portrait and wedding photographers: Lightroom AI masking (eliminates manual masking for skin, sky, subject), Aftershoot or Narrative Select for culling, and Luminar Neo's portrait AI for retouching. For wildlife and sports photographers: Topaz Photo AI for noise reduction at high ISOs and AI culling for large burst volumes. For landscape photographers: Lightroom AI sky mask, Luminar Neo Sky AI for sky replacement, and Photoshop Generative Fill for object removal.

Universal starting points for all photographers: enable Lightroom's AI masking and use the Auto button more aggressively as a starting point. These are free within existing Adobe subscriptions and provide immediate time savings. Most photographers find Lightroom AI Denoise alone justifies the Creative Cloud subscription through improved image quality and time savings.

### How good is AI noise reduction compared to traditional methods?

AI noise reduction is dramatically better than traditional noise reduction sliders for most practical scenarios. Traditional noise reduction (Lightroom's Luminance slider) reduces noise by blurring detail - effective for grain but destructive to fine texture. AI noise reduction (Topaz Photo AI, Lightroom AI Denoise) distinguishes noise from detail using learned models, reducing grain while preserving or even recovering fine detail.

For images shot at ISO 1600-3200, Lightroom AI Denoise typically produces excellent results that traditional methods cannot match. For extreme high ISO (6400+), Topaz Photo AI is generally considered the strongest option. The quality improvement is most visible in wildlife photography with long lenses in low light, event photography at ISO 3200-6400, astrophotography, and any image where grain is objectionable while fine detail (fur texture, feather structure, fabric weave) must be preserved.

### Is AI sky replacement ethical in photography?

Sky replacement ethics depend entirely on context and the photographer's contract with clients and publishers. In commercial photography (real estate, advertising, lifestyle), sky replacement is widely accepted and clients often expect it. In photojournalism, documentary photography, and competition photography where rules prohibit it, sky replacement violates the factual representation standard.

The practical guidance: be clear with clients about your retouching approach, follow competition rules explicitly, and never present composited elements as documentary photography. For most portrait, wedding, and commercial photographers, sky replacement is simply part of the post-processing toolkit. For photojournalists, the standard is unambiguous - sky replacement is not acceptable.

### How does Topaz Photo AI compare to Lightroom AI Denoise?

For most photographers shooting at ISO 800-3200, Lightroom AI Denoise and Topaz Photo AI produce comparable results that are both significantly better than traditional noise reduction. The differences become more apparent at extreme ISO levels.

Topaz Photo AI advantages: stronger performance at extreme high ISO (6400-51200), better detail recovery on certain camera sensors, combined workflow for noise reduction plus sharpening plus upscaling in one step. Lightroom AI Denoise advantages: integrated into Lightroom workflow without plugin switching, free for Lightroom subscribers, handles most practical noise situations effectively.

For photographers regularly shooting wildlife at ISO 6400+, astrophotography, or indoor sports at extreme ISO, Topaz Photo AI's additional quality is worth the cost. For portrait, wedding, and landscape photographers who rarely push past ISO 3200, Lightroom AI Denoise is typically sufficient.

### How much time can AI culling save per wedding or event?

Photographers using AI culling tools consistently report culling time reductions of 60-80% for large events. A wedding photographer who previously spent 5 hours culling 2,500 frames to 500 selects reports 1-1.5 hours with AI culling - the AI pre-selects to 600-700, and the photographer reviews and trims to the final 500.

The time savings compound across a busy season. For a photographer shooting 25 weddings per year, AI culling recovering 3.5 hours per wedding represents 87.5 hours of recovered professional time annually. At any reasonable professional hourly rate, this justifies AI culling tool subscription costs many times over and directly addresses one of the most significant quality-of-life challenges in event photography.

### Is AI sky replacement ethical in photography?

Sky replacement ethics depend entirely on context and the photographer's contract with clients and publications. In commercial photography (real estate, advertising, lifestyle), sky replacement is widely accepted and clients often expect it. In photojournalism, documentary photography, and competition photography where rules prohibit it, sky replacement violates the factual representation standard that defines these genres. Most professional journalism organizations explicitly prohibit sky replacement and treating composited images as documentary photographs.

In fine art photography, the question is personal - whether the image represents itself as a documentary record or as a creative composite affects the ethical analysis. Be transparent with clients and publications about your retouching approach, and never present composited elements in contexts where they would be understood as documentary.

### How good is AI noise reduction compared to traditional methods?

AI noise reduction is dramatically better than traditional noise reduction sliders for most practical scenarios. Traditional noise reduction (Lightroom's Luminance slider) reduces noise by blurring detail - effective for grain but destructive to fine texture. AI noise reduction (Topaz Photo AI, Lightroom AI Denoise) distinguishes noise from detail using learned models, reducing grain while preserving or even recovering fine detail.

For images shot at ISO 1600-3200, Lightroom AI Denoise typically produces excellent results that traditional methods cannot match. For extreme high ISO (6400+), Topaz Photo AI is generally considered the strongest option. The quality improvement is most visible in: wildlife photography with long lenses in low light, event photography at ISO 3200-6400, astrophotography, and any image where grain is objectionable.

### Is AI sky replacement ethical in photography?

Sky replacement ethics depend entirely on context and the contract between photographer and subject or publication. In commercial photography (real estate, advertising, lifestyle), sky replacement is widely accepted and clients often expect it - the goal is an appealing image, not a documentary record.

In photojournalism, documentary photography, and competition photography (when competition rules prohibit it), sky replacement violates the factual representation standard that defines the genre. Most professional journalism organizations explicitly prohibit sky replacement. In fine art photography, the question is personal - whether the image represents itself as a documentary record or as a creative composite.

The practical guidance: be clear with clients about your retouching approach, follow competition rules explicitly, and never present a composited sky as documentary photography.

### How does Topaz Photo AI compare to Lightroom AI Denoise?

For most photographers shooting at ISO 800-3200, Lightroom AI Denoise and Topaz Photo AI produce comparable results that are both significantly better than traditional noise reduction. The differences become more apparent at extreme ISO.

Topaz Photo AI advantages: stronger performance at extreme high ISO (6400-51200), better detail recovery on certain camera sensors, combined workflow for noise reduction + sharpening + upscaling in one step. Lightroom AI Denoise advantages: integrated into Lightroom workflow without plugin switching, free for Lightroom subscribers, handles most practical noise situations effectively.

For photographers regularly shooting wildlife at ISO 6400+, astrophotography, or indoor sports at extreme ISO, Topaz Photo AI's additional quality is worth the cost. For portrait, wedding, and landscape photographers who rarely push past ISO 3200, Lightroom AI Denoise is typically sufficient.

### What practical advice helps photographers evaluate whether AI tools are worth buying?

The tool evaluation framework for photographers considering AI tools:

**Test before buying:** Most reputable AI photography tools offer free trials. Process a representative sample of your most challenging images through the trial version - the images that most need what the tool claims to provide. Evaluate honestly against your current workflow results.

The colorization is based on learned color associations (grass is green, sky is blue, flesh tones follow learned patterns) and produces plausible if not historically precise results. For family history projects, AI colorization transforms historical photographs into something more immediately emotionally resonant.

**Facial restoration in damaged portraits:** GFPGAN and similar face restoration models can restore severely degraded facial details in portraits - recovering recognizable faces from photographs that have been creased, faded, or damaged in ways that obscure facial features.

**Stabilization for historical film:** For photographers working with historical film footage as well as still photography, AI video stabilization and enhancement tools (Topaz Video AI) can significantly improve the quality of historical footage.

Photo restoration services are a meaningful revenue opportunity for photographers with AI tool skills, particularly serving family and local history markets where the emotional value of restored photographs is high relative to the cost of AI-assisted restoration.

### What does the professional photography community think about AI tools?

Professional photography community responses to AI tools are diverse, reflecting the range of photography specialties and values:

**Wide adoption in commercial and applied photography:** Commercial photographers, real estate photographers, product photographers, and wedding photographers have largely embraced AI tools as professional efficiency tools. The attitude in these communities is pragmatic - AI tools that improve client outcomes and business economics are adopted without significant resistance.

**Significant debate in art and documentary photography:** Fine art photographers, photojournalists, and documentary photographers have deeper debates about what constitutes acceptable AI enhancement. Professional organizations including the National Press Photographers Association have taken clear positions against generative AI in photojournalism, while acknowledging traditional digital darkroom techniques as acceptable.

**Competition community standards:** Photography competitions have varying standards. Some competitions now explicitly disqualify AI-enhanced or AI-generated images, while others are developing disclosure requirements. Photographers who enter competitions should check current rules carefully, as standards are actively evolving.

**Education and technique community:** Photography educators are grappling with how to teach traditional techniques alongside AI tools. The consensus forming in professional education: students should understand both traditional techniques and AI tools, with traditional technique providing the foundation that makes AI tool use intelligent rather than blind.

**The generational dimension:** Younger photographers who learned their craft alongside AI tools tend to have fewer reservations about AI integration. Photographers who built their skills and reputations on traditional technique mastery have more complex relationships with tools that reduce the value of that mastery.

The professional photography community is not monolithic on AI tools. The appropriate position depends on your specialty, values, and the expectations of your clients and publications. Understanding the norms in your specific photography community helps you make informed decisions about how you present your AI-enhanced work.

### How do photographers handle AI-generated images in their portfolio versus traditional photography?

As AI image generation tools improve, photographers face questions about presenting AI-generated content alongside traditional photography:

**Clear separation is generally appropriate:** For professional photography portfolios and client-facing materials, clearly distinguishing between photographed images and AI-generated images respects client expectations and professional standards. Presenting AI-generated images as photography misrepresents the service being offered.

**AI as concept visualization, not portfolio content:** Using AI-generated images as pre-shoot concept visualization (showing clients the intended aesthetic before the shoot) is widely accepted. Including these AI concept images in the final portfolio presentation without clear context would be misleading.

**Creative AI work as a separate practice:** Some photographers are building separate portfolios of AI-generated work that they present as digital art, distinct from their photography practice. This transparency allows clients to engage with either practice (or both) with accurate expectations.

**AI-enhanced photography vs. AI-generated photography:** Lightroom AI masking, AI noise reduction, and similar AI-assisted traditional photography processing are generally considered photography, not AI generation. The distinction is between AI tools that enhance photographic capture and AI tools that generate images from text prompts without a photographic source.

**Market-specific expectations:** Different markets have different standards. Commercial and advertising clients expect significant post-processing; photojournalistic clients expect minimal processing. Understanding your market's expectations guides appropriate disclosure practices.

The photography profession is actively developing norms around these questions. Photographers who lead with transparency about their process - whatever it includes - build client relationships based on genuine understanding of what they are purchasing, which creates more sustainable professional relationships than obscuring the role of AI in the work.
