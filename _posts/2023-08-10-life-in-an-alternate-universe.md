A speculative walk through the choices not made — the jobs declined, the cities not moved to, the conversations that ended differently — and what that thought experiment reveals.

## The thought experiment

Pick a moment where you made a significant decision. Now imagine you made the opposite choice. Not fantasizing about a better outcome — just following the thread honestly, including the costs.

## What the exercise reveals

The alternate universe version of yourself is not a better version. It is a differently constrained version. The problems change shape; they don't disappear. This is not pessimism — it's a useful correction to the assumption that a different path would have been easier.

## Why it's worth doing occasionally

It builds appreciation for the actual path, not through forced gratitude but through honest accounting. The life you have is not the only possible life, but it is real — and that gives it a weight the imagined alternatives can never have.
