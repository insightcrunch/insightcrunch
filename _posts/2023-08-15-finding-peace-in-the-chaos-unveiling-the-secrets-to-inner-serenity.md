Stillness is not the absence of noise. It is a relationship with noise — and a practiced capacity to not be governed by it.

## The misunderstanding

Most approaches to inner peace treat it as a state to be achieved and then maintained. This is the wrong model. Stillness is not a destination — it's a practice that generates its own momentum.

## What actually works

The research on attention and equanimity points consistently in one direction: the people who manage noise best are not those who have removed it from their lives, but those who have changed their relationship to it. They notice the impulse to react without acting on it.

## The practical version

Pick one moment each day that usually triggers irritation. Notice the irritation before you act. That's it. Over weeks, the gap between stimulus and response gets longer. That gap is where stillness lives.
