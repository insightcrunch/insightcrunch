## Tableau Data Analyst Certification Exam Pattern

Take the practice exams of the Tableau Data Analyst Certification above to get a complete understanding of what type of questions you can expect. It is important to practice with a variety of datasets so that you do not get accustomed to only one particular type of question. The first topic is connect and transform data, out of which you will be getting about 24% of the questions. The second topic is exploring and analyzing data, from which you will be getting 41% of the questions in the Tableau Data Analyst Certification exam. The third topic is creating content, from which you will be getting about 26% of the questions. The fourth topic is publishing and managing content on Tableau server and Tableau online, from which you will be getting about 9% of the questions.

It's extremely important for the Tableau Certified Data Analyst Certification exam aspirants to understand the purpose of the exam. We are evaluating your skills as a data analyst using these exams. Hence, your expertise as a Server administrator might not be that much important, but you should be excellent at using Tableau Desktop. The majority of the questions in the Tableau Certified Data Analyst Certification exam is focused on Tableau Desktop. Hence, you should be extremely familiar with using Tableau Desktop and the different functionalities of the tool. Regarding the other topics on Tableau Server and Tableau Online, you will be asked questions to see if you know the best practices on your visualization on the server, instead of questions from a server administrators standpoint.
